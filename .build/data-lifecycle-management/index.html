Data Lifecycle Management

An organization manages its data through the data lifecycle. This document describes a possible data Lifecycle model.

This document is based on [1] and deviates from [1] for the purpose of brevity, clarity, and generality.

Data Lifecycle Phases

The phases of the data lifecycle considered by this document are Identify, Use, Maintain, and Dispose. Not all data lifecycle phases occur for every datum.

  • Identify: The organization identifies datums.
  • Use: The organization accesses, views, shares, and modifies datums. New datums may be created by aggregation or disaggregration of existing datums. Datums may also be repurposed.
  • Maintain: The organization preserves datums over time. This may include converting datums to different formats or representations as technologies change so it will continue to be usable.
  • Dispose: The organization disposes a datum at the end of its data lifeccle. The datum that is no longer needed or is required to be disposed is destroyed or otherwise made unavailable to unauthorized parties.

Structured, Semi-Structured, and Unstructured Data

Structured data has a known model that determine access and interpretation of the data. The opposite is unstructured data which has no known model associated that determines access or interpretation of the data. Between unstructured and structured data resides semi-structured data which has a known model associated which allows a certain degree of access and interpretation - however, the model associated that allows full access or interpretation is not known.
